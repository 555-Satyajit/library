About Us

At a Glance

The concept of Harekrushna Mahtab State Library was conceived during the 1st Five Year Plan under the advice of Government of India and was completed in 1959 with a majestic building of around 16000 sq.ft. carpet area, situated on a land of 3 acres in the capital city of Bhubaneswar.

In 1967, the building was named Gandhi Bhawan to commemorate the birth centenary of Mahatma Gandhi. In 1987, the building was renovated and fully utilized for the functioning of both the State Library of Odisha and the Bhubaneswar Public Library.

The institution was later renamed Harekrushna Mahtab State Library in memory of Dr. Harekrushna Mahtab, the builder of modern Odisha.
