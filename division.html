ORGANIZATION OF ODISHA STATE LIBRARY

From the organizational point of view, the Odisha State Library functions through structured library units and service divisions to ensure systematic administration, technical processing, and reader-focused services.

State Library (SL)

Provides reference materials and research support to scholars, researchers, and government departments.
Houses government publications, official records, and statutory documents for reference and preservation.
Acts as a legal depository library for publications received under statutory provisions.
Maintains a comprehensive collection of regional, national, and international language and literature resources.

Bhubaneswar Public Library (BPL)

Dedicated reading and learning resources designed for children.
Accessible materials and services for visually impaired readers.
Daily newspapers and periodicals for public access and reference.
Subject-wise collections covering general and competitive study areas.
Career-oriented books and guidance materials for students and job seekers.
Donated and curated personal collections preserved for public reference.
Educational and informational audio-visual resources.
Photocopying and document reproduction services for readers.
Book preservation and binding services to maintain collections.

Administrative Services Division

Manages staff administration, service records, and personnel matters.
Handles financial management, budgeting, and audit-related activities.
Oversees procurement, inventory, and material management.
Maintains official registers and internal issue tracking records.

Technical Services Division

Responsible for selection and procurement of library resources.
Implements and maintains library automation systems.
Digitizes rare and important documents for preservation and access.
Coordinates national and metadata-related library initiatives.
Manages activities related to Raja Rammohan Roy Library Foundation.

Reader Services Division

Provides direct library services to members, researchers, and the general public.
