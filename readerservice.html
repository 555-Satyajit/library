Readers Service

Comprehensive library services and organizational structure of HKM State Library

Organisational Pattern of HKM State Library

From the organisational point of view the State Library has been divided into three main Divisions, namely, Administrative Services Division (ASD), Technical Services Division (TSD) and Reader Services Division (RSD).

Administrative Services Division (ASD)

ASD deals with all such matters connected with administration, establishment, finance, accounts, maintenance, etc.

Technical Services Division (TSD)

TSD deals with all technical activities pertaining to book selection, acquisition, classification, cataloguing, conservation, binding, etc.

Besides purchase, books are also received as gratis under the Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867 and from Raja Rammohun Roy Library Foundation, Kolkata as well as other gifts.

To record acquisitions, the library has adopted a multiple accession register system designed by Prof. D.R. Kalia that reveals total collection of the library in consolidation as well as collection development taken place in different sections separately.

Reader Services Division (RSD)

RSD deals with Registration of members, Organization of documents, Compilation of Odisha State Bibliography, reference and referral services, circulation of books and periodicals services, reprography services etc.

State Library (SL) Sections

1. Research and Reference Section

Reference Collection

All reference materials published on various subjects and those of National and International publications are arranged subject wise on the lines of DDC Class Numbers in this Section to facilitate Reference Studies for both general and research scholars.

The documents constitute Dictionaries (Language and Subject), Directories, Bibliographies, Handbooks, Yearbooks, Manuals, Encyclopedias, Guidebooks, maps, atlases, Multi volume sets published by National and International agencies.

The Library holds a comprehensive collection of reference materials for the benefit of the reading public of Odisha as other small public Libraries functioning in different localities of the State are not viable enough to procure these materials.

Area Studies Collection

A classification Schedule for Area Studies has been devised by Prof. D.R.Kalia to help classifying documents under Area Studies collection.

The Area number of a specific place, country or region chosen from the schedule is only superimposed over the class number of the documents concerned so that all documents dealing with that place, country or region are brought together under the specific Area Number and form Area Study Collection.

To facilitate researcher doing research on different aspects of Odisha (that is on socio-economic and cultural aspects, political situation and conditions, scientific and technological developments, History and geography etc), the Reference and Research Section of HKM state Library stocks and organizes all such documents published so far on the State together under Area studies collection.

For comparative study and supplementary reading it also gathers materials on those aspects of other areas of the country as well as the world. These collections are named as Odisha Collection, National Collection and International Collection.

In recent times one can feel the importance of Area studies. People are travelling too much for recreation, business, education and jobs due to globalization. To know about that locality they are asking for books of that particular region instead of subject.

National Collection

Books dealing with India are organized and then the collections of different States are organized to facilitate comparative study with the state of Odisha.

International Collection

Books dealing with world are organized and then the collections of different countries are organized to facilitate comparative study with the state of Odisha.

Odisha Collection

Titles published on or about any aspect of Odisha irrespective of their place of publication are brought together under the Area Number for Odisha.

A special collection under this section is developed known as "Jagannath Cult and Culture".

Tribal Collection

The Library gathers all documents related to tribes of India in one section with special focus on tribes of Odisha.

Fine Art Collection

Valuable pictorial materials on architecture, sculpture, folk arts, photography, music, sports, recreational and performing arts are organized here.

Women Studies Collection

Interdisciplinary documents on women and gender studies are gathered together without scattering them by class numbers.

Biography Collection

Biographies are grouped under Class Number “B” and divided into National Biography, International Biography and Odisha Biography.

Subdivisions include BA-Autobiography, BI-Individual Biography and BC-Collective Biography.

Official Documents Collection

Government publications including reports, census, budgets, gazettes, legislative documents and notifications are organized here.

Corporate Collection

Publications of United Nations, UGC, Government Sponsored autonomous agencies and research institutes are organized separately as primary sources of information.

2. Depository Section

One copy of every book published in Odisha is preserved here under the Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867.

The first copy goes to Depository Section for preservation and compilation of Odisha State Bibliography and the second copy goes to Reader Services Division for use.

3. Language and Literature Section

Odia, English, Hindi, Sanskrit, Bengali and Urdu language and literature collections are available here.

The section supports both HKM State Library and Bhubaneswar Public Library users.

Bhubaneswar Public Library (BPL) Sections

1. Children Section

Children between 5–14 years have access to books, magazines, educational toys, multimedia and reading facilities.

2. Text Book & Career Development Section

Curriculum-based and competitive examination materials are organized here and this section serves nearly 70% of users.

3. Subject Collection Section

General subject books for supplementary reading and general interest.

4. Serials Section

Newspapers and periodicals in Odia, English and Hindi are provided along with preserved copies for reference.

5. Braille Section

Braille books and magazines for visually impaired readers, including gifts received from USA.
